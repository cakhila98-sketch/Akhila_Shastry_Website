Weight Loss Journey
Ongoing · Updated Monthly

This is the most honest thing on this page. The weight loss journey is not a transformation story — it is a work in progress. I am documenting every month: what I ate, how I moved, where I failed, and what I learned. There is no before and after here, only the middle. I believe that showing up imperfectly, consistently, is worth more than any dramatic result. This record exists to hold me accountable and to remind me that the process is the point.

Swimming
National Level · Backstroke · CBSE · 2012–2016

I grew up in the water. Swimming at national level — earning the Best Swimmer title at CBSE Saurashtra Zone and placing 4th in the National IM Relay — taught me what it means to train for something larger than a single moment. Backstroke was my style: you move forward without seeing where you are going, trusting your instincts and your training. I have not competed in years, but the water is still home. Getting back is on the list.

Hatha Yoga
Coming Soon · On the List

Hatha yoga has been on my mind for a while — the slow, deliberate practice of holding postures, building strength through stillness. Unlike power yoga, Hatha moves at a pace that forces you to pay attention. I am drawn to it because of how naturally it connects to the inner work I already do — Reiki, Vipassana, meditation. The body and the breath as one practice. I plan to begin formally and document it here when I do.

Power Lifting
Coming Soon · A Goal in Progress

Power lifting is a goal I have been circling for a while. There is something deeply appealing about a sport built entirely on how much you can lift — no aesthetics, no style points, just raw strength and technical precision. The squat, the bench, the deadlift. I want to compete eventually, but first I want to build the foundation properly, with a coach, with patience, without shortcuts. This section will come alive when I begin.

Trekking
Trails & Elevation

There is a particular kind of clarity that only comes from moving through difficult terrain on foot. Trekking strips everything back — no signal, no schedule, just the next step and the view it earns you. I have done a handful of trails and each one has left me quieter and more grounded than I arrived. The mountains have a way of putting the rest of life in perspective. More trails are planned. More is always planned.

Golf
Patience as a Practice

Golf is a sport that demands more patience than most people are willing to give. Every shot is a negotiation between your body, your mind, and the conditions in front of you. I am drawn to it for exactly this reason — it rewards the long game in every sense. The learning curve is real and humbling, and that is precisely what makes it interesting. A sport where you are always competing against yourself first.

Why it matters
The body keeps the score.

Sport is not separate
from everything else.

Physical discipline is the foundation I build everything else on. Sport taught me how to lose well, train without guaranteed outcomes, and show up when the motivation is gone. Sportsmanship — respecting the game, the opponent, and yourself — is a philosophy that transfers to every boardroom, every negotiation, every hard conversation. I believe that how you move through the world physically shapes how you move through it in every other way. The body and the mind are not separate projects.
